## Craft Requirements

### Voice and Tone
- Match the voice profile stated in the project context exactly
- Write in an authoritative but human register — confident without being arrogant
- Avoid the stilted, listing quality of AI-generated prose (do not write "Firstly... Secondly... Thirdly...")
- Avoid hollow openers like "In today's world," "It goes without saying," or "As we all know"

### Sentence Rhythm
- Vary sentence length deliberately. Short sentences land claims. Longer sentences carry the reader through nuance, qualification, and the development of complex ideas before arriving at a clear resolution.
- Never write three sentences of the same length in a row
- Use the em dash, the colon, and the semicolon — sparingly but with purpose
- Avoid starting more than two consecutive sentences with the same word

### Paragraph Structure
- Open each paragraph with its controlling idea or a compelling hook
- Develop claims with evidence, examples, or narrative — never assertion alone
- End paragraphs with either a resolved thought or a forward-pulling tension
- Keep paragraphs between 80 and 200 words unless structural emphasis demands otherwise

### Concreteness
- Every abstract claim must be grounded with at least one specific example, case, or illustration
- Prefer the particular over the general: "a 1987 study at Stanford" beats "research has shown"
- Use sensory language when describing real-world scenes or processes
- Vivid, original metaphors are welcome — clichéd metaphors are not

### What to Avoid
- Padding and throat-clearing: get to the point
- Redundant summaries within the same section
- Hedging phrases that drain authority ("it could be argued," "some might say")
- Jargon not previously defined for this audience
- Any phrase listed in "Phrases Already Used"

### Continuity
- If prior sections are listed, do not re-introduce concepts already established
- Reference prior content briefly if needed, but do not summarize it at length
- The section must feel like it belongs to the same book as what came before
